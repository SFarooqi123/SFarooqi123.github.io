Aligning R/G/B channels from historical glass negatives to reconstruct full-color photographs.

OverviewWhat & Why

The Prokudin-Gorskii collection stores three monochrome exposures (B, G, R) captured through color filters. To recover a color photo, we must register the channels so that the same physical scene points align across layers. This write-up summarizes my pipeline, shows results on the provided images, includes a few of my own, and documents failures and enhancements.

Deliverables covered below: overview & approach, results on example images with offsets, additional images, brief failure analysis, bells & whistles with before/after, and a note on submitting to the gallery.

ApproachAlignment Strategy & Implementation Notes

I treat the blue plate as the static reference and align green and red to it. For small images I perform an exhaustive search over integer shifts in a window (e.g., ±15 px). For larger images, I use a pyramid (image pyramid with downsampled levels), refining from coarse to fine to keep runtime manageable.

  • Similarity metric: NCC/SSIM (toggleable) after cropping borders to avoid edge artifacts.
  • Pre-processing: intensity normalization; optional Sobel-edge alignment for texture-poor scenes.
  • Search: coarse-to-fine displacements with a small local refinement at the final scale.
  • Post: slight auto-contrast; optional white balance; border trimming.

If an image struggled, I tried: increasing crop %, switching to edge-based NCC, or expanding the coarse search radius.
